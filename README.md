📁 Project 1: Investment Banking & M&A Analysis (Best Buy)
Overview

This project simulates a buy-side Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A) analysis where an advisory team evaluates Best Buy Co., Inc. as a potential acquisition target.

The analysis follows a typical investment banking workflow including company profiling, financial modeling, comparable company valuation, and strategic analysis.

The objective is to help senior leadership determine whether Best Buy represents an attractive acquisition opportunity and to support strategic acquisition decision-making.

🎯 Objectives

Evaluate Best Buy’s financial and strategic position.

Build forward-looking financial projections to estimate future company performance.

Estimate company valuation using market-based comparable company analysis.

Analyze Best Buy’s growth strategy and long-term value drivers.

Present insights in executive consulting-style formats to support leadership decision making.

🧩 Project Structure
Task 1 — Target Company Profile

A consulting-style company profile was developed to summarize the key characteristics of Best Buy for senior management review.

Key areas analyzed include company overview, leadership structure, enterprise value estimation, and strategic developments.

Company overview analysis included year founded, store network size, revenue generation, EBITDA performance, and the company’s overall business model in the consumer electronics retail sector.

Enterprise value analysis focused on market capitalization, debt levels, cash position, and estimation of enterprise value to understand the company’s financial scale.

Leadership analysis reviewed key executives including the CEO, CFO, Chief Technology Officer, and Chief Customer Officer to understand management structure and strategic leadership.

Strategic developments were evaluated including the Best Buy Totaltech subscription program, the transition toward a recurring revenue model, and initiatives aimed at strengthening customer loyalty and service-based revenue.

Task 2 — Financial Forecasting Model

A five-year income statement forecasting model was built to estimate Best Buy’s future financial performance.

The financial model included the complete income statement structure such as revenue, cost of goods sold, gross profit, SG&A expenses, EBITDA, depreciation and amortization, EBIT, interest expenses, taxes, and net income.

Key assumptions were applied to project future performance including revenue growth assumptions, cost structure assumptions, operating expense ratios, and tax rate projections.

Financial modeling principles included dynamic Excel formulas, assumption-driven forecasting, linked financial calculations, and logical financial statement flow.

The model provides forward-looking projections that serve as inputs for valuation models such as discounted cash flow analysis and M&A pricing discussions.

Task 3 — Comparable Company Analysis (CCA)

A comparable company valuation analysis was performed using peer companies within the retail sector.

Comparable firms included major retailers operating in similar market segments.

The analysis involved calculating enterprise value multiples and equity valuation multiples to benchmark Best Buy against industry peers.

Key valuation metrics included enterprise value to revenue multiples and enterprise value to EBITDA multiples, as well as price-to-earnings ratios.

The valuation process included calculating market capitalization, determining net debt, computing enterprise value, calculating valuation multiples for each peer company, and determining average and median sector valuation benchmarks.

These benchmarks were then applied to Best Buy’s financial metrics to estimate the company’s implied market valuation.

Task 4 — Strategic Growth Analysis

Best Buy’s investor presentations and corporate strategy materials were analyzed to identify long-term growth drivers and strategic initiatives.

The analysis focused on key themes such as increasing technology demand, expansion into new product categories, subscription ecosystem development, and omnichannel retail strategy.

Technology demand growth reflects increasing adoption of connected devices and digital lifestyles.

Expansion into new categories includes growth areas such as health technology, fitness technology, outdoor living solutions, and electric mobility products.

Subscription ecosystem initiatives focus on Totaltech membership, enabling recurring revenue generation and improved customer retention.

Omnichannel retail strategy integrates physical stores, e-commerce platforms, virtual consultations, and in-home services to deliver a seamless customer experience.

A key strategic insight from the analysis is that Best Buy is transitioning from a traditional electronics retailer toward a technology solutions platform.

📁 Project 2: Financial Analysis & Risk Monitoring (Banking / FP&A)
Overview

This project simulates the role of a Financial Planning and Analysis (FP&A) analyst working at a global bank.

The project focuses on analyzing financial performance, evaluating regulatory capital adequacy, monitoring liquidity risk, forecasting potential financial risks, and analyzing consumer spending behavior.

The objective is to generate data-driven insights that help senior management understand the bank’s financial performance and risk exposure.

🎯 Objectives

Analyze year-over-year financial performance to identify revenue drivers and profitability trends.

Evaluate capital adequacy under Basel III regulatory requirements.

Monitor liquidity risk using early warning indicators.

Assess consumer spending behavior using macroeconomic data.

Provide insights that support executive financial decision-making.

🧩 Key Analytical Areas
Financial Performance Analysis

A year-over-year financial performance analysis was conducted to understand changes in revenue, operating expenses, and net income.

Revenue growth was driven primarily by higher interest rates, increased loan balances, and strong performance in institutional financial services.

Operating expenses declined due to cost control measures and reduced corporate consulting costs.

However, credit provisions increased significantly due to reserve builds and normalization of credit losses following unusually low levels during earlier periods.

As a result, despite revenue growth, overall profitability declined due to higher credit costs.

Basel III Capital Adequacy Analysis

Capital adequacy was evaluated using Basel III regulatory requirements to ensure the bank maintains sufficient capital relative to its risk exposure.

The capital adequacy ratio measures the relationship between a bank’s core capital and its risk-weighted assets.

Basel III regulations require banks to maintain a minimum capital adequacy ratio of 10.5 percent.

Risk-weighted assets were calculated by applying different regulatory risk weights to various asset categories including credit card loans, consumer mortgages, retail banking exposures, and unsecured retail lending.

The analysis identified certain operating units that fell below the regulatory capital requirement, indicating potential compliance and financial stability risks.

Consumer Spending Analysis

The project analyzed the relationship between COVID-19 case trends and credit card spending behavior.

Spending data was segmented into different categories including social spending, delayable purchases, staple purchases, and work-related spending.

The analysis revealed that during COVID case surges, discretionary spending categories such as social activities and delayable purchases declined significantly.

Staple spending remained relatively stable because consumers continued to prioritize essential goods.

Work-related spending declined due to reduced commuting and remote work trends.

An important insight was that consumer spending tends to recover quickly once pandemic conditions stabilize, suggesting that spending declines are often temporary rather than permanent.

Liquidity Risk Monitoring

Liquidity risk was monitored using internal early warning triggers and management action triggers designed to detect financial stress before it escalates.

Early warning triggers included conditions such as significant deposit withdrawals, rapid increases in loan balances, and declining liquidity buffers.

Management action triggers represent critical conditions where the bank’s liquidity position becomes negative, meaning loans exceed deposits.

Such conditions require immediate treasury intervention to restore liquidity stability.

This framework enables banks to proactively manage liquidity risks and maintain financial resilience during periods of market stress.
